Original Verse Explanation

7 Isaac spoke up and said to his father Abraham, “Father?” “Yes, my son?” Abraham replied. “The fire and wood are here,” Isaac said, “but where is the lamb for the burnt offering?” 8 Abraham answered, “God himself will provide the lamb for the burnt offering, my son.” And the two of them went on together.

Wow this verse is kinda sad. So what's happening here is that Abraham had his long-awaited and promised son and a few years later God asked him to literally give his son as a sacrifice, and knowing Abraham that was a lot because he and his wife were already very old and wouldn’t be able to have another child to carry Abraham’s legacy. Even with that, Abraham was steadfast and went through with what God asked of him without even questioning because what God gave He can take back. Another view of what was going on in Abraham’s mind was that he knew how powerful God is, and therefore knew he was able to bring people back to life, and he knew that God would do that because He promised Abraham that Isaac would be his legacy, and Abraham would be the father of Nations so there was no way that his legacy (Isaac) would die and God would do nothing about it because then God would’ve broken His promise and He doesn't do that. And in this verse, they are literally walking to Isaac’s death, and his son Isaac asks his father where is the lamb for the offering because they had everything besides that. This is not in the verse but turns out that God came through and right when Abraham was putting his son on the stone to start the burnt offering, a lamb appeared and God told him to use that lamb, so he was testing Abraham’s loyalty to him and he passed because Abraham was willing to give his only son to God, just like God would do later on for the world.
