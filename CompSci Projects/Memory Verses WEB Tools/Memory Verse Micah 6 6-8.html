Micah 6:6-8

Original Verse

Micah 6:6–8 (NIV): 6 With what shall I come before the LORD and bow down before the exalted God? Shall I come before him with burnt offerings, with calves a year old? 7 Will the LORD be pleased with thousands of rams, with ten thousand rivers of olive oil? Shall I offer my firstborn for my transgression, the fruit of my body for the sin of my soul? 8 He has shown you, O mortal, what is good. And what does the LORD require of you? To act justly and to love mercy and to walk humbly with your God.

Explanation of the Verse

This verse is trying to make us reflect on what God asks of us, and that is mainly the idea of loving Him and others the way He loves you, to try doing the right thing, and being humble wherever you go. He says that at the end of the verse, the rest is then built by using examples of what people would usually do to “clean” themselves and be a little bit worthy of God’s love back in the time of Micah. A good example used is Abraham and his son when the verse talks about offering the firstborn for their transgressions. Other examples include what they would most likely do and that is offers some of their animals to God, especially calves, which is also a representation of Jesus's death for us because he is the lamb that died so we could be “clean” from sin if we decided to walk with Him. With that, the verse is trying to tell us that the way to please God is to maybe offer Him something that matters to you nowadays like giving up your phone for some time and/or using your life to be an instrument of God’s work and spread his gospel to other people.
